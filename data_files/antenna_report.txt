TECHNICAL REPORT  
Design and Structural Analysis of a Directional Yagi-Uda Antenna  
Prepared by: Hayao Miyasaki  
Date: May 14, 2025  

Page 1: Introduction

1.1 Purpose  
This report provides a detailed technical analysis and structural design of a directional Yagi-Uda antenna for long-range VHF communication applications. The design is tailored for outdoor mounting and uses aluminum and composite materials for durability and weight management.

1.2 Scope  
The focus is on optimizing antenna gain, directivity, and mechanical robustness while adhering to manufacturing standards. The report covers dimensions, material thicknesses, element spacing, and mounting design.

1.3 Operating Frequency  
The antenna is designed for a center frequency of 144 MHz, corresponding to a half-wavelength of approximately 40.9 inches. All physical dimensions are derived from this base frequency.

Page 2: Mechanical Overview

2.1 Boom Design  
The boom is the main support structure:  
- Length: 72 inches  
- Cross-sectional shape: square tube  
- Outer dimensions: 1 inch x 1 inch  
- Wall thickness: 0.125 inches  
- Weight: approx. 2.5 lbs per 36 inches  

Mounting holes are drilled every 6 inches for flexible element positioning.

2.2 Element Layout  
The Yagi design includes:  
- 1 Reflector  
- 1 Driven Element  
- 4 Directors  

Element lengths and positions:  
- Reflector: 42.25 inches (positioned 8 inches from rear)  
- Driven Element: 40.9 inches (centered at 16 inches)  
- Director 1: 39.5 inches (at 24 inches)  
- Director 2: 39.0 inches (at 32 inches)  
- Director 3: 38.75 inches (at 40 inches)  
- Director 4: 38.5 inches (at 48 inches)  

Spacing between directors: 8 inches  

All elements are made from 0.375-inch diameter solid aluminum rods, with a center-mounting bracket extending 1.5 inches below the boom.

Page 3: Material Specifications

3.1 Element Materials  
Elements are cut from 6061-T6 aluminum:  
- Tensile strength: 45,000 psi  
- Each rod length tolerance: ±0.0625 inches  
- Surface finish: brushed, with anodizing for corrosion resistance  

Ends of each element are capped with polyethylene tips, each 0.75 inches long and 0.5 inches in diameter.

3.2 Mounting Brackets  
Brackets are stainless steel (grade 304):  
- Plate thickness: 0.125 inches  
- Width: 2 inches  
- Height: 3 inches  
- Hole diameter: 0.25 inches  
- Distance between mounting holes: 1.75 inches  

3.3 Insulator Blocks  
Used to isolate the driven element:  
- Width: 1 inch  
- Height: 1.5 inches  
- Depth: 0.75 inches  
- Mounting slot: 0.3125 inches wide, 0.5 inches deep  

Page 4: Electrical & Performance Parameters

4.1 Feed Point Design  
Driven element is center-fed using a coaxial feed:  
- Connector: SO-239  
- Center pin solder depth: 0.25 inches  
- Feed point impedance: 50 ohms  
- Balun length: 8 inches (1:1 choke balun)  
- Coax loop diameter: 4 inches  

4.2 Tuning Adjustments  
End sleeves provide fine-tuning:  
- Sleeve length: 2 inches  
- Adjustment range: ±1 inch  
- Locking set screw: 0.125 inches diameter, 0.25 inches long  

4.3 Expected Performance  
- Forward gain: ~11 dBi  
- Front-to-back ratio: 20 dB  
- SWR bandwidth (2:1): 2.5 MHz  
- SWR at center frequency: 1.2:1  
- Effective boom length: 72 inches  

Page 5: Mounting & Environmental Tolerance

5.1 Mast Mount Assembly  
The antenna mounts to a vertical mast:  
- U-bolt diameter: 0.5 inches  
- Saddle bracket: 3 inches wide  
- Mast compatibility: 1 inch to 2 inches diameter  
- Clearance above roofline: 24 inches  
- Minimum separation from obstacles: 36 inches  

5.2 Wind Load and Safety  
Rated for wind speeds up to 90 mph:  
- Projected area: 120 square inches  
- Weight (assembled): 4.8 lbs  
- Center of gravity from rear: 28 inches  
- Boom flex under wind load: max 0.5 inches  
- Recommended guy wire anchor spacing: 48 inches apart  

5.3 Temperature Tolerance  
Operational range: -40°F to 140°F  
Expansion tolerance of boom: ±0.25 inches over 72 inches  

Page 6: Manufacturing and Assembly

6.1 Assembly Process  
- Tools required: hex driver, torque wrench  
- Recommended torque on all fasteners: 15 inch-pounds  
- Assembly time: approx. 90 minutes  
- Boom end caps: 1 inch diameter, 1.25 inches deep  

6.2 Inspection Checklist  
- Element alignment tolerance: ±0.125 inches  
- Element spacing deviation: max ±0.0625 inches  
- Driven element alignment to boom centerline: within 0.25 inches  
- All fastener extensions below surface: less than 0.125 inches  
- Visual inspection of solder joint depth: 0.25 inches minimum  

6.3 Conclusion  
This Yagi-Uda antenna design combines efficient signal transmission with robust structural integrity. With 50 precise measurements in inches and careful material selection, it supports VHF communication with high gain and reliable performance in harsh environments.
